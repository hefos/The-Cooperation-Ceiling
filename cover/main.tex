Dear Professor Chaplain, Professor Sasaki, and Professor Weitz,

Co-Chief Editors, \emph{Journal of Theoretical Biology}

We are pleased to submit our manuscript ``The Ceiling of Cooperation in
Extrinsic Update Rules'', by Harry Foster, Vince Knight, and Sebastian Krapohl,
for consideration in the \emph{Journal of Theoretical Biology}.

The emergence of cooperation is a central question at the heart of evolutionary
game theory. Classical evolutionary dynamics typically allow individuals to
update their strategies based only on the relative success of available actions,
as in the Moran process or imitation dynamics. More recently, alternative update
rules have been proposed that compare an individual's payoff against other
benchmarks. Notably, aspiration dynamics (Du et al., 2014) compare payoffs with
a personal aspiration level, while introspection dynamics (Couto et al., 2022)
compare realised payoffs with the counterfactual payoff that would have been
obtained by playing an alternative strategy.

Building upon an established framework, we propose a classification of these
processes as ``purely extrinsic'' or ``purely intrinsic''. We then prove a
result that under purely extrinsic population dynamics, the abundance of
cooperation in social dilemmas cannot exceed the neutral drift baseline.
Finally, we illustrate this result in a heterogeneous public goods game, and
identify the conditions under which purely intrinsic population dynamics are
able to overcome it.

The paper is presents a unified comparison between four population dynamics,
with two representative update rules in each category. This provides two
contributions: first in the formulation of a formal classification of update
rules; and second, the derivation of an upper bound on the abundance of
cooperation in purely extrinsic update rules. These results apply across a
number of evolutionary processes, and thus we believe that the manuscript is
well suited to the \emph{Journal of Theoretical Biology}.
